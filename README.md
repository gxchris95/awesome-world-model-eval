As they scale, the hard question shifts from *"can it generate a plausible-looking future?"* to *"is that future physically correct, geometrically consistent, controllable, and useful for a task?"* This list collects the benchmarks and metrics that try to answer it.

## Why Evaluate World Models?

A recurring, independently-replicated finding across the 2025–2026 literature: **visual realism does not imply physical plausibility, geometric consistency, executability, or task success.** Models that top perceptual leaderboards (FID/FVD/VBench) routinely violate conservation laws, break under viewpoint changes, freeze when the camera looks away, or fail to drive a policy to its goal. Reported by [World-in-World](#video--pixel), [WorldArena](#embodied--policy-evaluation), [WorldLens](#driving), [RoboWM-Bench](#embodied--policy-evaluation), [CRONOS](#video--pixel), and [Out of Sight](#video--pixel), among others.

The frontier of evaluation is therefore moving **from "does it look right?" to "does it work?"** — toward functional, closed-loop, and causal tests.

## Evaluation Dimensions

Every metric here sits on **two independent axes** — *what* it measures and *how* it's scored. Keeping them separate is the key to reading the field: a physics metric and a fidelity metric can each be scored automatically, by a VLM judge, or by humans.

**What is measured** — five families (lower rows are the harder, more discriminative tests the field is moving toward):

| Dimension | What it measures | Typical metrics | Typical scoring |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Visual fidelity** | Per-frame / clip realism | FVD, FID, LPIPS, SSIM/PSNR, MUSIQ, VBench | automatic |
| **2. Consistency & persistence** | Coherence over time, views, memory | DINO/CLIP consistency, reprojection error, World Stability, rFID, InterStab | automatic + specialist-vision |
| **3. Controllability & action fidelity** | Does output follow the action / instruction? | trajectory ADE/FDE/DTW, camera-pose ATE/RPE, instruction-following, `1−LPIPS` | automatic (SLAM, tracking) |
| **4. Physics & causality** | Physical-law adherence, counterfactuals | physical-commonsense, per-law scores, Physics-IQ, counterfactual sensitivity | VLM-judge + human |
| **5. Functional / closed-loop** | Real task usefulness | task success / SPL, policy-eval correlation, OOD generalization | closed-loop rollout |

**How it's scored** — the orthogonal axis (the *Typical scoring* column shows the dominant choice, but most dimensions can use any): `automatic` (pixel/feature computation) · `specialist-vision` (a pretrained model — DINO, CLIP, MUSIQ) · `VLM-judge` (a VLM rates outputs) · `human` study · `closed-loop` (run the predicted world as an environment). The [Metric Glossary](#metric-glossary) lists the specific reusable metrics grouped by these same five dimensions.

## Metric Glossary

The specific, reusable metrics behind the benchmarks above, **grouped by the same five [Evaluation Dimensions](#evaluation-dimensions)** (what they measure). The **Scoring** column is the orthogonal *how* axis. Only metrics that recur across this list are included; **Used by (of 63)** is *how many of the 63 benchmarks* use the metric (breadth of adoption — not how often it's computed), followed by a few example benchmarks. (Generic video-generation metrics that barely appear here — e.g. Inception Score, FVMD — are omitted on purpose.)

#### 1 · Visual fidelity
| Metric | What it measures | Scoring | Defined in | Used by (of 63) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **FVD** — Fréchet Video Distance | clip-level distributional realism | automatic | Unterthiner '18 | 7 — EVA, LoopNav, OmniWorld, DrivingGen |
| **FID** — Fréchet Inception Distance | image-set distributional realism | automatic | Heusel '17 | 3 — SimWorld, WorldEval |
| **LPIPS** | perceptual frame similarity | automatic | Zhang '18 | 6 — World Stability, World-in-World, MIND |
| **SSIM / PSNR / MSE** | structural & pixel-level fidelity | automatic | Wang '04 / classical | 6 — WorldGym, LoopNav, Wow-wo-val |
| **MUSIQ** (imaging quality) | no-reference image quality | specialist-vision | Ke '21 | 6 — WBench, MIND, WorldMark, iWorld-Bench |
| **LAION aesthetic** | human-preference visual appeal | specialist-vision | Schuhmann '22 | 11 — WorldScore, WBench, WorldMark |
| **VBench** (suite) | multi-axis perceptual quality | automatic | Huang '23 | suite — WorldModelBench, PhyWorld, Omni-WorldBench |

#### 2 · Consistency & persistence
| Metric | What it measures | Scoring | Defined in | Used by (of 63) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Temporal consistency / flicker / motion-smoothness** | frame-to-frame stability over time | automatic | per-paper | 17 — WorldModelBench, WorldScore, WBench |
| **Subject / Background consistency** | object & scene identity preserved across frames | specialist-vision | per-paper | 8 — WorldSimBench, EVA, WBench, WorldArena |
| **DINO / DINOv2 cosine consistency** | semantic feature stability across frames | specialist-vision | Oquab '23 | 5 — World Stability, DrivingGen, Wow-wo-val |
| **Reprojection / geometric residual** | 3D structural coherence across views | automatic | geometry-based | 8 — WorldScore, WBench, CRONOS, PDI-Bench |
| **Optical-flow / photometric consistency** | appearance stability under motion | automatic | per-paper | 8 — WorldScore, WBench, 4DWorldBench |
| **Specialized stability scores** (World Stability, rFID, InterStab) | consistency after action↔inverse, scene re-visit, or interaction | automatic | per-paper | World Stability, Mechanistic View, Omni-WorldBench |

#### 3 · Controllability & action fidelity
| Metric | What it measures | Scoring | Defined in | Used by (of 63) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Trajectory error** (ADE / FDE / DTW / nDTW / Hausdorff) | predicted vs. intended path | automatic (tracking) | std.; Ilharco '19 (nDTW) | 8 — EWMBench, ACT-Bench, iWorld-Bench, Beyond-Sim |
| **Camera-pose error** (ATE / RPE) | camera / agent trajectory accuracy | automatic (SLAM) | SLAM-based | 3 — OmniWorld, MIND, WBench |
| **Instruction / action following** (incl. IEC) | did the model execute the commanded action | automatic + VLM-judge | per-paper | 5 — WorldModelBench, ACT-Bench, WorldArena |
| **1 − LPIPS controllability** | predicted vs. real observation under an action | automatic | World-in-World | 1 — World-in-World |

#### 4 · Physics & causality
| Metric | What it measures | Scoring | Defined in | Used by (of 63) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Physical commonsense / adherence** | does motion obey real-world physics | VLM-judge + human | per-paper | 20 — WorldModelBench, VideoPhy-2, PhyWorld, Physion-Eval |
| **Per-physical-law score** | adherence split by law (gravity, collision, fluids…) | VLM-judge / human | per-paper | 15 — WorldModelBench, PhyWorld, PhyGround |
| **Spatial / Spatiotemporal IoU** | where & when a physical event occurs vs. real footage | automatic | classical (IoU) | 7 — Physics-IQ, WorldBench, Newton |
| **Counterfactual sensitivity** | output change under controlled interventions | automatic | per-paper | 2 — CRONOS, MentalMap |

#### 5 · Functional / closed-loop
| Metric | What it measures | Scoring | Defined in | Used by (of 63) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Task Success Rate / SPL** | completes the actual downstream task | closed-loop | Anderson '18 (SPL) | 19 — World-in-World, WorldSimBench, RoboWM-Bench |
| **Policy-eval correlation** (Pearson / Spearman / MMRV) | agreement with real policy performance or human ranking | automatic | per-paper | 18 — WorldGym, WorldEval, WorldArena |
| **OOD generalization** | robustness to visual / geometric / physical shift | closed-loop / automatic | per-paper | 11 — stable-worldmodel, WM-ABench, MIND |
| **Executability / IDM Turing test** | predicted frames → valid real-robot actions | closed-loop | per-paper | 3 — RoboWM-Bench, Wow-wo-val, Text2World |
| **Route completion / driving score** | long-horizon driving success | closed-loop | per-paper | 3 — WorldSimBench, WorldLens, WorldArena |

> **On `VLM-judge` scoring:** a vision-language model rates outputs — used by **13/63** benchmarks (e.g. WorldModelBench, PhyWorld, WorldReasonBench, STEVO-Bench), most heavily for the physics and instruction-following metrics above.

> **Are these metrics trustworthy? — validation against humans.** The strongest benchmarks calibrate their automatic / VLM scores against human ratings and report the agreement: **WBench** Spearman ≥0.94 · **Wow, wo, val!** Pearson >0.93 · **WorldMark** ρ>0.9 · **WorldArena** r=0.825.
